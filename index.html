Issa Rae

“Black women aren’t bitter, they’re just tired of being expected to settle for less.”
Jo-Issa Rae Diop
  • 1985 - Born in Los Angeles, California
    • Father: Dr. Abdoulaye Diop - A pediatric doctor from Senegal
    • Mother: Delyna Diop (née Hayward) - A teacher from Louisiana
    • Rae and her four siblings grew up in Darkar, Senegal and Potomac, Maryland
  • Graduated from King Drew Magnet High School of Medicine and Science where she started acting
  • 2007 - Graduated from Stanford University with a major in African and African-American Studies
    • Made music videos and directed plays
    • Meet Tracy Oliver who helped produce Awkward Black Girl
  • Received a theater fellowship at The Public Theater in New York City
  • 2011 - Web Series Awkward Black Girl premiered on YouTube
    • Partnered with Pharrell and premiered the second season on his channel iamOTHER
  • 2012 - Included on the annual Forbes '30 Under 30' list in the entertainment section
  • 2013 - Awkward Black Girl won a Shorty Award for Best Web Show
  • 2015 - Published memoir The Misadventures of Awkward Black Girl and became New York Times best-seller
  • 2016 - Release of Insecure Season 1 on HBO
  • 2017 - American Film Institute selected Insecure as one of the top 10 television series of the year
  • 2017 - Release of Insecure Season 2 on HBO
  • 2017-2018 - Rae received two Golden Globe nominations for Best Actress - Television Series Musical or Comedy

I've always had an issue with the [assumption] that people of color, and black people especially, aren't relatable. I know we are.

-Issa Rae
